About Me

I am Someshwaran G, a Research Scholar and Assistant Professor with a strong academic background and a deep interest in advanced computing technologies. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Image Processing using Deep Learning at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Kattankulathur, where my research focuses on developing intelligent and reliable solutions for real-world problems, particularly in the healthcare domain. I hold a Master’s degree in VLSI Design and a Bachelor’s degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering. These qualifications have provided me with a strong foundation in both hardware and software systems, enabling me to approach research problems with a multidisciplinary perspective and a strong analytical mindset.

My core areas of specialization include Artificial Intelligence, Image Processing, Computer Vision, Deep Learning, and VLSI Design. I am actively involved in research, teaching, and student mentoring, with hands-on experience in applying advanced deep learning models and convolutional neural networks to medical image analysis and AI-driven healthcare applications. I have authored and co-authored multiple peer-reviewed journal and conference publications in reputed international venues. I am passionate about knowledge dissemination, academic excellence, and innovation-driven research, and I continuously strive to contribute to technology-enabled solutions that create meaningful impact in society while mentoring and inspiring the next generation of engineers and researchers.

Publications

Peer-reviewed journal articles and international conference publications focusing on medical image processing, deep learning, and fetal heart disease detection.

Someshwaran G, Sarada V. (2025).
“FHD Deep Learning Prognosis Approach: Early Detection of Fetal Heart Disease Using Ultrasonography Image-based IROI Combined Multiresolution DCNN.”
Technology and Health Care – Official Journal of the European Society for Engineering and Medicine.
This work presents an advanced deep learning-based prognosis framework for early-stage fetal heart disease detection using ultrasound images. The model integrates IROI extraction with multiresolution DCNN to enhance diagnostic accuracy.

Someshwaran G, Sarada V. (2024).
“Disease Prognosis of Fetal Heart’s Four-Chamber and Blood Vessels in Ultrasound Images Using CNN Incorporated VGG-16 and Enhanced DRNN.”
International Arab Journal of Information Technology (IAJIT), 21(6).
This study proposes a hybrid CNN-DRNN architecture combining VGG-16 features for accurate analysis of fetal heart chambers and vascular structures in ultrasound imagery.
